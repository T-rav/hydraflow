# Book Roadmap: One Story, Three Books

Captured 2026-06-11, from the working session that added the Cherny "write loops"
positioning and the 2025-papers lineage to the talk. This note exists so the thread
doesn't get lost between the talk (June 24) and the fall.

The shape: a single documented experiment told in the order it happened, with
receipts at every stage. Three books, one arc: **practice, phenomenon, meaning.**

---

## Book One: *Vibing to Prod* (written, capture draft)

**Subtitle:** Vibe Coding and the New Grammar of Software
**Status:** 259-page capture draft exists (PDF, complete prose, full TOC, synopsis,
epilogue). Needs heavy editing. Capture was the point; the raw truth is down.

**Arc:** Old Grammar craft (HyperCard at twelve, TDD, distributed systems) →
creation turns conversational → InsightMesh V1 collapse → the accidental lab
(2,500 conversations, the 34%, coherence theory) → what works (loops beat prompts,
gravity anchors, the Vibe-Forge-Mature rhythm) → InsightMesh v2 as proof case →
epilogue: "the future belongs to those fluent in the grammar of intent."

**Key decision: do NOT extend this book with the factory.** Its arc is complete
(confidence → collapse → investigation → recovery → rhythm). Bolting a HydraFlow
Part VI on would break a finished shape and invalidate its own resolution. The
factory is a sequel premise, not a next chapter.

**Editorial flags for the fall pass:**

1. **The 34% means different things in different artifacts.** The case study
   defines it as sessions that exited after the first response. The book says
   "34 percent of accepted outputs were false positives." Materially different
   claims wearing the same number. Pick the precise sentence once and propagate
   it across book, papers, and the talk's receipts slide.
2. **Vocabulary concordance needed across the corpus.** Old Grammar / New Grammar,
   Vibe-Forge-Mature, gravity anchors, AI Decision Loop, FLUID, V2V, dark factory,
   walls. Some are the same concept in different clothes (gravity anchors ≈ the
   container). Decide canonical terms vs deprecated aliases. The factory breaks
   the build when code and glossary drift; the bookshelf needs the same CI.
3. **Ration the staccato.** Single-line paragraphs hit hard in the collapse
   chapters (the best writing in the book) but fatigue across 259 pages. Vary the
   gait in calm chapters; save the drumbeat for the wounds.
4. **Parts IV and V drift from memoir toward manual.** Decide which chapters earn
   their place in the story vs belonging in the living docs the factory already
   maintains. Structural edit before line edit.

**Publishing plan:** self-publish. Own the pipeline, control the gates, ship when
ready (dark factory mode for books). Non-negotiable gate: a professional human
**developmental editor first** (structure, pacing, the Parts IV/V decision), then
a copy editor. Self-publishing without an editor is vibing to prod with no forge.
Find an editor with tech-memoir experience; the voice is the asset to protect.
Do not rush the book to hit June 24; the talk is the demand signal, not the
deadline. Email capture at the talk via aibuddy.software/papers ("the book is
coming"). Option worth keeping: versioned releases, errata as patches, the
concordance as a living appendix the factory maintains.

## Book Two: *When Code Becomes Fluid* (unwritten; the talk is its proposal)

**Status:** not started, and can't be finished yet: its third act is still
running (gate erosion's outcome, the Z experiment, whether the container holds).
The June 24 GOTO talk is effectively the book's thesis statement and market test.
Watch what the room leans into; Q&A and the dinner conversation are the chapter
outline, delivered free by a curated audience.

**Why this title:** names the phenomenon rather than the machine, so it survives
HydraFlow being superseded. Already accruing equity (essay, talk, stage video all
feed the phrase). Carries the protagonist shift: Book One was about the author
changing; this title says the material changed.

**Content:** the factory story. HydraFlow's birth and leaving the nest in three
days, the no-review bet, walls over vigilance, quality moving from social to
structural, the caretaker taxonomy that named its own categories, the scar night,
MockWorld, gate erosion, the peer constellation (Yegge, Cherny, Huntley,
Steinberger, StrongDM), and Z as the ending the talk can only promise.

**The seam between the books is load-bearing.** Book One's epilogue lands on
"the grammar of intent." HydraFlow's tagline is "intent in, software out." Book
One ends on the sentence Book Two opens with.

**The spine of the two-book arc:** Book One's deepest wound is on its own page:
"In InsightMesh V1, architecture lived in me... The moment a system depends on a
single person's memory to remain coherent, it is already dead." Being the
architecture was the failure. Book Two's thesis is the cure: project the
architecture out of the head and into walls, gates, and loops, until the system
doesn't need its author at all. **The factory is the projection** of the author's
craft: the discipline, the distrust of unverified certainty, the restraint
encoded as policy. Book One: the system died because it lived in my head.
Book Two: so I moved out of my head, and it stopped needing me.

**Sequencing:** edit Book One through the fall while the factory accrues Book
Two's third act on its own. By the time Book One ships, there's a talk reception
plus months more of factory receipts to write from.

## Book Three: *Lessons in Language* (someday; the synthesis)

**Status:** years out by design. The evidence is still being generated, and not
by the author: every label trail, wiki regeneration, term proposal, and pruning
is Book Three's primary source material, journaled nightly by the factory itself.

**Why this title here and not on Book Two:** it's the umbrella over the whole
body of work, not an engine-noise title. Language is the actual through-line:
Book One is subtitled "the New Grammar of Software," the coherence paper is about
how meaning gets reconstructed, and the factory's most distinctive machinery is
linguistic (ubiquitous language as living artifact, glossary drift as CI failure,
terms proposed and pruned by loops, ADRs and scenarios as the load-bearing layer
while code stays disposable). The deepest claim nobody else is making: **in a
system where code is fluid, language is the load-bearing layer.** Book Three is
the one that says what it all meant, written after the experiments report back
(Z's game, gate erosion, concepts as runtime objects).

**Form option:** Book Three may be operated rather than written: a living-artifact
publication the factory keeps current as experiments keep reporting. A book about
language as the durable layer, kept durable by the machinery it describes. The
form would be the argument.
